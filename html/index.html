PC Master Race

What is it and what does PC Master Race mean?

"PC Master Race" is a term that refers to a subculture of gamers and computer
enthusiasts who advocate the use of personal computers (PCs) as the superior
gaming platform. Members of the PC Master Race argue that PCs offer a better
gaming experience due to their customizability, greater processing power and
graphics, and the availability of a wide range of exclusive and third-party
game titles.

The term "PC Master Race" originated as a joke on the Reddit forums in 2011,
but has since become a popular and, in some cases, controversial term in the
gaming and tech community. Some have criticized the elitist and condescending
attitude some members of the PC Master Race take towards console gamers or those
who can't afford to build or upgrade a high-end PC.

How was the PC Master Race born?

The origin of the term "PC Master Race" can be traced back to a Reddit gaming
forum thread in 2011. The user named "pedro19" wrote in response to a comment
about the superiority of game consoles: "The Game console is a decent platform,
but PC games will always be better? Why? Because PC games have better graphics,
more control, and are less expensive."

In response to this comment, another user named "Artemisx" responded with a joke,
writing "PC Master Race!" as a claim that PC gamers were superior to console
gamers. The term quickly caught on in the Reddit community, and other users began
using it as a way of asserting the superiority of the PC gaming platform.

PC Gamer

The beginnings of the PC Gamer go back to the 1980s, when the first personal
computers such as the IBM PC and Apple II began to gain popularity in the consumer
market. In the beginning, PC games were simple and mostly limited to text games
or text based adventure games. However, as personal computer technology improved,
so did PC gaming.

In the 1990s, the rise of real-time strategy games and role-playing games for PCs,
such as Command & Conquer, Warcraft, and Diablo, helped fuel interest in PC gaming.
Furthermore, the popularity of network and online gaming also contributed to the
growth of the PC Gaming community.

In the 2000s, the advent of first-person shooters like Half-Life and Counter-Strike,
as well as the increasing use of 3D graphics, brought the PC gaming experience
to the next level. The availability of low-cost, indie games through services like
Steam has also helped increase the popularity of PC gaming in recent years.

Today, the PC Gamer has become a subculture of gamers and computer enthusiasts
who uphold the superiority of PCs as the ultimate gaming platform. The PC Gaming
community remains active and vibrant, with a wide range of cutting-edge games,
hardware, and technology available to those seeking the best gaming experience.

Types of PC Gamer

There are different types of PC Gamer depending on the needs, budget and preferences of each player. Some of the more common PC Gamer types include:

  1. Low-end PC: These are inexpensive systems that offer basic
    performance for casual or less demanding games. These systems often
    use low-end processors and graphics cards, and may not be easily
    upgradeable or upgradeable.

  2. Mid-Range PCs: These are solid-performing systems that offer a
    smoother gaming experience on popular game titles. These systems
    typically use mid-range processors and graphics cards, and may
    have some ability to upgrade or upgrade in the future.

  3. High-end PCs: These are high-end systems that offer the best
    performance and the best gaming experience in the most demanding
    and next-generation games. These systems typically use high-end
    processors and graphics cards, and can be highly upgradeable and
    expandable.

  4. PC de realidad virtual (VR): son sistemas diseñados específicamente
    para soportar la realidad virtual, ofreciendo una experiencia
    inmersiva en juegos y aplicaciones de realidad virtual. Estos
    sistemas suelen utilizar componentes de hardware de alta gama
    para garantizar un rendimiento suave y sin problemas.

  5. Gamer Laptop PC: These are portable systems that offer solid
    gaming performance and the convenience of being able to take them
    anywhere. These systems are typically more expensive and may have
    limited battery life, but offer the advantage of being portable.

  6. Custom Desktop PCs: These are customized systems that are built to
    the user's specifications. These systems offer a great deal of
    customization and the ability to choose specific components to
    optimize performance and the gaming experience.

In summary, there are many types of PC Gamer available, and the choice
will depend on the needs, budget and preferences of each gamer.
